History of Barangay Villahermosa

    History of Villahermosa

    by: Renz Ury Cordero

    Villahermosa is a barangay located in the province of Albay's municipality of Daraga. It is known as the boundary of Albay and Sorsogon district. Its population is consisting of 3,128 residents. According to elders, Villahermosa is known for its entertainment during the Marcos regime. It had a bowling alley and cinema for the people. The land is rich in crops, and large houses and buildings were constructed by a Marcos-type building back in the old days.

    Carabaos were commonly used as transportation of farmers and can be seen roaming around the road. The cows' manure is commonly seen by people. There were lots of trees along the road and a procession of elders and kids usually in the time of holy week. The people stop at the Our Lady of the Holy Rosary for the mass, and foods were distributed to people for free.

    Villahermosa was recognized as the headquarters for the 901st infantry battalion and served as a training site for future police officers in the region. During the 1900s, the military regularly patrolled the area with tanks and infantry. According to an elder named "Encinares," she recalls witnessing a significant presence of armed forces and the enforcement of curfews during her childhood. She also stated that Villahermosa was almost considered as a municipal instead of Daraga, but the building operation was stopped as soon as Marcos was expelled from the presidency.
